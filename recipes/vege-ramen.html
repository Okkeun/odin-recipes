Vege Spicy Miso Ramen

This recipe is not mine. Original source of photo and recipe belongs to:

rozkoszny

Ingredients


    Broth & Base

  • 3 sweet potatoes (sliced, unpeeled)
  • 1/3 cup + 4 tbsp oil
  • 5 cloves garlic (sliced)
  • tbsp sesame seeds
  • 3 tsp chili flakes (divided: 2 + 1)
  • 2 shallots (chopped)
  • 5 cm ginger (cubed)
  • 1 ½ tbsp tomato paste
  • 15 dried shiitake mushrooms (approx. 30g)
  • 3 tbsp white miso paste (shiro)
  • 1 ¼ liters cold water
  • 2 tbsp cold butter (or soy cream for vegan version)
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce


    • Toppings & Noodles

  • 400g ramen noodles
  • 4 eggs
  • 3 pak choy heads
  • Mung bean sprouts
  • Nori sheets (optional)

Preparation

  1. Roast Potatoes: Toss sliced sweet potatoes with 2 tbsp oil and salt. Bake at 220°C (430°F) for 25 mins until charred in spots.
  2. Chili-Garlic Oil: Sauté garlic in 1/3 cup oil until golden (2-3 mins). Add sesame seeds and 2 tsp chili flakes; fry for 1 min. Remove from heat and set aside in a bowl.
  3. The Base: In a pot, sauté shallots and ginger in 2 tbsp oil until soft (5 mins). Add 1 tsp chili flakes (30 sec), then tomato paste. Fry for 2-3 mins until it darkens.
  4. The Broth:Add shiitake, 1 tbsp miso, and water. Bring to a boil, then remove from heat. Let it sit covered for 20-30 mins.
  5. Finish Soup: Blend the broth (with mushrooms) until smooth. Stir in the remaining 2 tbsp miso and whisk in cold butter. Season with soy sauce and salt.
  6. Sides: Boil eggs for 7.5 mins (soft boil), then cool in ice water and peel. Blanch pak choy for 1-2 mins. Cook noodles according to package instructions.
  7. Assembly: Place noodles and pak choy in bowls. Pour over the broth. Top with halved eggs, roasted sweet potatoes, sprouts, nori, and a drizzle of the garlic-chili oil.
